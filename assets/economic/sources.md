# Austrian Primary-Source Mapping

This file maps the principles in this folder to canonical Austrian authors and representative primary works.

## Method and Foundations

- **Action axiom / praxeology**
  - Ludwig von Mises, *Human Action* (1949)
  - Ludwig von Mises, *Epistemological Problems of Economics* (1933)

- **Methodological individualism**
  - Carl Menger, *Principles of Economics* (1871)
  - Ludwig von Mises, *Human Action* (1949)
  - F. A. Hayek, *The Counter-Revolution of Science* (1952)

- **Subjectivism of value / ordinal utility**
  - Carl Menger, *Principles of Economics* (1871)
  - Eugen von Böhm-Bawerk, *Capital and Interest* (1884–1909)

## Capital, Time, and Interest

- **Capital heterogeneity / production structure**
  - Eugen von Böhm-Bawerk, *Capital and Interest* (especially Book V)
  - F. A. Hayek, *Prices and Production* (1931)
  - F. A. Hayek, *The Pure Theory of Capital* (1941)

- **Time preference / originary interest**
  - Eugen von Böhm-Bawerk, *Positive Theory of Capital* (1889)
  - Ludwig von Mises, *Human Action* (1949)

- **Roundabout production**
  - Eugen von Böhm-Bawerk, *Positive Theory of Capital* (1889)
  - F. A. Hayek, *Prices and Production* (1931)

## Prices, Knowledge, and Coordination

- **Prices as signals / dispersed knowledge**
  - F. A. Hayek, “The Use of Knowledge in Society” (1945)
  - F. A. Hayek, *Individualism and Economic Order* (1948)

- **Market process / discovery**
  - Israel M. Kirzner, *Competition and Entrepreneurship* (1973)
  - Ludwig von Mises, *Human Action* (1949)

- **Spontaneous order / catallaxy**
  - F. A. Hayek, *Law, Legislation and Liberty* (Vol. 1, 1973)
  - F. A. Hayek, *Individualism and Economic Order* (1948)

## Money, Cycle, and Calculation

- **Monetary non-neutrality / Cantillon-type effects**
  - Richard Cantillon, *Essay on Economic Theory* (c.1730; precursor, widely used in Austrian analysis)
  - Ludwig von Mises, *The Theory of Money and Credit* (1912)

- **Austrian business cycle theory (credit distortion, malinvestment)**
  - Ludwig von Mises, *The Theory of Money and Credit* (1912)
  - F. A. Hayek, *Prices and Production* (1931)
  - Murray N. Rothbard, *America’s Great Depression* (1963)

- **Economic calculation argument**
  - Ludwig von Mises, “Economic Calculation in the Socialist Commonwealth” (1920)
  - F. A. Hayek, “The Use of Knowledge in Society” (1945)

## Property, Institutions, and Intervention

- **Property rights and coordination preconditions**
  - Carl Menger, *Principles of Economics* (1871)
  - Ludwig von Mises, *Human Action* (1949)

- **Interventionism and process distortions**
  - Ludwig von Mises, *Critique of Interventionism* (1929)
  - Ludwig von Mises, *Human Action* (1949)
  - Murray N. Rothbard, *Man, Economy, and State* (1962)

---

## Notes on Scope

- Included works are selected for canonical status within Austrian economics.
- Dates are publication dates of standard editions/original publication windows.
- This mapping is conceptual; many principles are developed across multiple Austrian texts.
