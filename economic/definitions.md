# Definitions (Austrian Economics)

## Action
Purposeful behavior: selecting means to pursue chosen ends.

## Ends and Means
Ends are desired states; means are scarce resources used to attain them.

## Scarcity
Condition in which available means are insufficient to satisfy all conceivable ends.

## Opportunity Cost
Highest-valued foregone alternative when a choice is made.

## Subjective Value
Personal valuation assigned by an acting individual; not an objective physical property.

## Ordinal Utility
Preference ranking without meaningful interpersonal or cardinal measurement.

## Marginal Utility
Value of an additional unit determined by its place in the actor’s preference order.

## Time Preference
Preference for present satisfaction over future satisfaction, ceteris paribus.

## Originary Interest
Intertemporal discount embedded in exchange between present and future goods.

## Capital Structure
Interconnected, time-ordered arrangement of heterogeneous capital goods used in production.

## Roundaboutness
Degree to which production uses longer, more indirect methods to increase output quality or quantity.

## Entrepreneur
Agent who exercises judgment under uncertainty and coordinates resources toward anticipated demand.

## Profit and Loss
Ex post signals of plan success or error relative to consumer preferences and opportunity costs.

## Economic Calculation
Comparative assessment of alternative resource uses via monetary prices.

## Market Process
Ongoing sequence of rivalrous bidding, discovery, error correction, and coordination.

## Spontaneous Order
Orderly pattern arising from decentralized interactions under general rules.

## Knowledge Problem
No central planner possesses the dispersed, tacit, time-sensitive knowledge held by individuals.

## Catallaxy
Hayek’s term for the market order produced by exchange among many agents.

## Money
Generally accepted medium of exchange that reduces barter frictions and enables monetary calculation.

## Credit Expansion
Increase in loanable funds through banking/monetary mechanisms not backed by prior real saving.

## Malinvestment
Investment in projects that appear profitable under distorted price/interest signals but are unsustainable.

## Natural Rate of Interest
Rate consistent with intertemporal preferences and real saving-investment conditions absent monetary distortion.

## Interventionism
Policy regime where market processes are partially constrained by controls, mandates, or discretionary state actions.

## Methodological Individualism
Principle that social outcomes must be explained through individual actions and interactions.
